Naguib Mahfouz:

Naguib Mahfouz, born in Egypt in 1911, won a Nobel Prize in Literature in 1988. He is one of the most recognized Egyptian writers, publishing numerous novels, short stories, journalism, memoirs, essays and screenplays. Since he was young, Mahfouz loved reading and writing and was inspired by the likes of Franz Kafka and James Joyce. Mahfouz is the only Arab to win the Nobel Prize in Literature.
